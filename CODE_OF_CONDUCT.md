# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by mistakes
- focusing on what is best for the overall community

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- public or private harassment
- publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Enforcement responsibilities are currently handled by the project maintainer through GitHub moderation tools.

The maintainer is responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior and may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, and other public project communication. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported through GitHub moderation and reporting tools. Do not use public issues to disclose sensitive personal information.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated as appropriate. The maintainer will respect the privacy and security of the reporter when handling an incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

The maintainer may use the following consequences depending on the severity and impact of the behavior.

### 1. Correction

Community Impact: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

Consequence: A private or public written warning, with clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

Community Impact: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

Consequence: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. The person may be asked to avoid interaction with involved people for a specified period.

### 3. Temporary Ban

Community Impact: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

Consequence: A temporary ban from interaction or public communication with the project for a specified period.

### 4. Permanent Ban

Community Impact: A pattern of violation of community standards, harassment of an individual, aggression toward classes of individuals, or other severe misconduct.

Consequence: A permanent ban from public interaction with the project.
